Democratizing
Fermentation.

We believe that making your own kombucha shouldn't require a science degree or expensive, hard-to-find ingredients. It should be a simple, beautiful ritual that connects you to your food and community.

"Our mission is to put a brewing jar on every kitchen counter in the world."

It started in a cramped apartment kitchen in Portland. Our founder, frustrated by the $5 bottles of store-bought kombucha and the confusing, contradictory advice online, set out to create a foolproof system.

We spent a year testing hundreds of tea blends, sugar ratios, and SCOBY cultures. We worked with microbiologists to isolate the most robust, resilient yeast and bacteria strains.

The result? The Kombucha Homebrew Supply. A curated ecosystem of lab-tested cultures, organic substrates, and the world's first localized Scoby Exchange platform.

Our Journey.

From a tiny kitchen to a global fermentation movement.

2021

The First Brew

Sarah begins brewing in her apartment, frustrated by inconsistent online advice. She starts keeping detailed lab notes on pH and temperature variables.

2023

Launching the Starter Kits

After perfecting the "Master Brewer" kit, we launched online. We sold out of our initial 500 kits in less than 48 hours.

2025

The Scoby Exchange is Born

Realizing shipping liquid cultures globally wasn't sustainable, we launched the Scoby Exchange—a platform connecting local brewers to trade cultures peer-to-peer.
