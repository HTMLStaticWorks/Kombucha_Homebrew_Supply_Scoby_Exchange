The Fermentation Timeline.

Understanding the microbial phases of kombucha brewing.

Days 1-3

Yeast Activation

Yeasts begin consuming sucrose, breaking it down into fructose and glucose, producing ethanol and CO2.

pH drops from 4.5 to 3.8
Pellicle formation visible
Days 4-7

Bacterial Acidification

Acetobacter converts ethanol into acetic acid. The distinct tart flavor profile develops and a new pellicle forms on the surface.

Days 8-14

Second Fermentation (F2)

Bottled with fruit or sugar. Anaerobic environment forces CO2 into the liquid, creating natural carbonation.

Target pH: 2.5 - 3.2
