Ginger Turmeric Immune Booster F2 Recipe

Sarah Jenkins
• Oct 12, 2025 • 5 Min Read

The transition from summer to fall means it's time to adjust our fermentation profiles. While berries are great for the heat, the cooler months call for roots. This Ginger Turmeric blend is not only deeply warming and earthy, but it packs a massive anti-inflammatory punch.

Why Ginger and Turmeric?

Ginger is a powerhouse in the second fermentation (F2) process. It contains natural yeasts on its skin that supercharge carbonation. If you've been struggling to get a fizzy brew, adding a teaspoon of fresh grated ginger is the easiest fix.

Turmeric adds a brilliant golden hue and earthy depth, complementing the tartness of the kombucha base. Remember to add a tiny pinch of black pepper—it contains piperine, which increases the bioavailability of turmeric's active compound, curcumin, by up to 2000%.

Pro Tip:

Always use fresh roots rather than powdered spices for F2. Powders tend to clump, float to the top, and can create a gritty texture in your final drink.

Ingredients (Per 16oz / 500ml Bottle)

  • 14 oz plain, unflavored Kombucha (from your F1)
  • 1 tsp freshly grated ginger (skin on is fine if organic)
  • 1/2 tsp freshly grated turmeric
  • 1 tsp organic cane sugar or honey (for carbonation food)
  • 1 tiny pinch of black pepper
  • 1 tsp lemon juice (optional, for brightness)

Instructions

  1. Prep the roots: Grate the ginger and turmeric. You don't need to peel them if they are organic; just wash them thoroughly.
  2. Bottle: Add the grated roots, sugar, black pepper, and lemon juice to your pressure-rated swing-top bottle.
  3. Pour: Gently pour your F1 kombucha into the bottle, leaving about 1 inch of headspace at the top.
  4. Seal and Ferment: Seal the bottle tightly and leave it at room temperature (ideally 75-80°F) for 2 to 4 days.
  5. Chill: Once you see bubbles at the top, move the bottle to the fridge for at least 12 hours before opening. This helps the CO2 dissolve into the liquid.

Strain the roots out as you pour, or leave them in for a rustic feel. Enjoy your immune-boosting brew!
